Kartike Swami
Temple

Devbhoomi Uttarakhand is home to many legends; some poplar while others not-so- often repeated, like the one about Lord Kartik. Perched on a hilltop near the village of Kanakchauri, Rudraprayag, the Kartik Swami temple is one of the most prominent ones dedicated to Lord Shiva's son Lord Kartik, in the state. Situated atop Kronch Parvat, the temple pays homage to Lord Kartik, also known as Lord Muruga or Lord Murugan in parts of India, and his devotion to his parents.

Kedarnath
Temple

Kedarnath, situated in the Rudraprayag district of Uttarakhand, India, is home to the renowned Kedarnath Temple, one of the twelve Jyotirlingas and a significant site for Hindu devotees. Surrounded by stunning snow-capped peaks and picturesque landscapes, this sacred pilgrimage destination holds cultural and religious importance. The temple, constructed in a traditional architectural style, attracts pilgrims seeking blessings and spiritual rejuvenation. Apart from its religious significance, Kedarnath's natural beauty and serene ambiance draw tourists and trekkers to explore its breathtaking trails and scenic vistas. .

Jim Corbit
National Park

Jim Corbett National Park, situated in the Nainital district of Uttarakhand, India, is renowned as the oldest national park in the country. Named after the renowned conservationist and author Jim Corbett, the park boasts a rich diversity of wildlife, including tigers, leopards, elephants, and numerous bird species. Its picturesque landscapes, encompassing hills, riverine belts, and grasslands, offer a captivating experience for nature enthusiasts and wildlife aficionados. The park serves as a prominent ecotourism destination, promoting wildlife conservation and environmental awareness.

Valley Of Flowers

The Valley of Flowers, nestled in the Chamoli district of Uttarakhand, India, is a breathtaking UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its vibrant meadows adorned with a rich variety of alpine flora. Embraced by the majestic Himalayas, this ethereal valley transforms into a kaleidoscope of colorful blossoms during the monsoon season, creating a mesmerizing panorama. Home to diverse species of flowers, including orchids and poppies, along with diverse fauna, the Valley of Flowers allures nature enthusiasts and trekkers seeking an enchanting and rejuvenating experience amidst its natural splendor.

Rishikesh
World Capital Of Yoga

Rishikesh, located in the foothills of the Himalayas in Uttarakhand, India, is renowned as a revered spiritual center and a prominent yoga hub. Nestled along the banks of the holy Ganges River, Rishikesh is esteemed for its ancient temples, ashrams, and spiritual retreats, attracting spiritual seekers and yoga enthusiasts from around the world. Additionally, it serves as a hub for adventure activities such as white-water rafting, trekking, and bungee jumping, offering a perfect blend of spiritual enlightenment and adrenaline-filled escapades amidst its picturesque natural surroundings.

Nature's Bounty

Untouched nature in all its splendour is what Uttarakhand offers visitors. The state has been blessed with gorgeous natural beauty. The fact that almost 45.43 per cent of its geographical area has a dense forest cover, makes it all the more remarkable. The state is host to the mighty Himalayas, which guarantee a rich growth of flora and fauna, a pleasant climate and a peaceful and serene environment. The many glaciers here are sources of some of the major rivers of India, including the Ganga and the Yamuna. Snow-covered mountain peaks, like Nanda Devi, Badrinath, and Kamet, offer stunning views, while oak, rhododendron, deodar, and pine forests leave one in awe.
